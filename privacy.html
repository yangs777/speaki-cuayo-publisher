Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 3, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Speaki Cuayo Publisher collects, uses, stores, and shares information when creators connect social media accounts and use the service to prepare, publish, and maintain short-form videos and related channel metadata.

1. Information we collect

We may collect account identifiers, profile names, profile images, authorization status, access tokens, refresh tokens, selected video files, captions, hashtags, titles, descriptions, tags, channel metadata, video metadata, comment metadata, reply drafts, posting settings, publishing results, error logs, and support messages that you provide or that are returned by connected platform APIs.

2. TikTok data and permissions

When you connect TikTok, the service may request permissions such as basic profile information and video upload or publishing permissions. These permissions are used to confirm the connected TikTok account, upload creator-selected videos, create drafts when available, publish content when requested, and show publishing status or errors.

3. YouTube data and permissions

When a YouTube or Google account is authorized, the service may request YouTube Data API permissions needed to read and update creator-owned channel information, video metadata, localizations, comment threads, and approved creator replies. YouTube data is used only for owner-authorized publishing, localization, comment triage, reply review, troubleshooting, and maintaining the connected YouTube channel.

The service uses the official YouTube Data API and Google OAuth flows. Use of YouTube data is also subject to the YouTube Terms of Service, YouTube API Services Terms of Service, YouTube API Services Developer Policies, and Google Privacy Policy.

4. How we use information

We use information to operate the publishing workflow, authenticate connected accounts, prepare posts, upload or publish videos selected by the creator, troubleshoot errors, maintain security, comply with platform requirements, and respond to support or deletion requests.

5. How we share information

We send video files, captions, metadata, posting options, and account authorization data to the connected platform only as needed to provide the publishing service. For YouTube, we send approved metadata updates, localization updates, and creator reply actions to YouTube only through official API endpoints. We do not sell personal information. We do not use connected account data for third-party advertising.

6. Storage and retention

Authorization tokens and publishing records may be stored in secure local or server-side environments used to operate the service. We keep information only as long as needed for publishing, troubleshooting, security, legal compliance, or user support, unless a longer period is required by law or platform requirements.

7. Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational safeguards designed to protect connected account data. No internet service can guarantee perfect security, so creators should also protect their own devices, passwords, and platform accounts.

8. User choices and deletion

You may revoke access through the settings of each connected platform. For Google and YouTube, you can revoke app access through your Google Account permissions. You may also request deletion of account data associated with the service by following the Data Deletion Instructions.

9. Children

The service is intended for creators who are old enough to manage social media accounts under the rules of the connected platforms. It is not directed to children.

10. Updates

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The updated version will be posted on this page with a new last updated date.
